What Perplexity Can and Cannot Do for SEO

Can do: Search intent analysis, topic gap research, content outline validation, People Also Ask question discovery, entity and topic mapping, competitor content summarisation, E-E-A-T signal research.

Cannot do: Keyword volume data, ranking tracking, backlink analysis, technical SEO auditing. Use Semrush, Ahrefs, or Google Search Console for these.

SEO Workflow 1: Search Intent Research

For any target keyword, query Perplexity: "What is the search intent behind the query '[keyword]'? What are users actually trying to accomplish, and what type of content would best satisfy their need?"

This gives you a nuanced intent analysis that goes beyond Google's "informational/navigational/transactional" categories — useful for deciding whether to write a guide, a comparison, a tool page, or a landing page.

SEO Workflow 2: People Also Ask Discovery

Query: "What are the 20 most common questions people ask about [topic]? Organise them by subtopic."

The resulting question list maps directly to: FAQ sections (FAQ schema for featured snippets), H2/H3 headings structure, and secondary keyword targets within the same article.

SEO Workflow 3: Competitor Content Gap Analysis

Query: "Summarise the top-ranking articles about [keyword]. What topics do they cover well? What important questions do they fail to answer?"

The gaps are your content opportunity — the angle that existing top-ranking content does not cover is exactly what will differentiate your article and give Google a reason to rank it above current results.

SEO Workflow 4: Topical Authority Mapping

For building topical authority (the SEO strategy of covering a topic exhaustively across multiple pages), query: "What are all the subtopics, related topics, and adjacent questions within the broader topic of [main subject]?"

This gives you a content cluster map — the hub article and all supporting articles needed to establish authority. A human-curated equivalent would take hours of manual research.

SEO Workflow 5: E-E-A-T Signal Research

Google's E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust) is increasingly important for ranking. Query: "Who are the most cited experts in [field]? What are their credentials, publications, and the platforms where they publish?"

Use this to identify: authors to quote, sources to link to, and experts to reference — all of which build E-E-A-T signals that help your content rank for competitive queries.

Best SEO stack for 2026: Perplexity (intent, gaps, entities, questions) + Semrush or Ahrefs (volume, KD, backlinks) + Google Search Console (your own ranking data) + Claude or ChatGPT (writing the optimised content). Each tool does what it is best at.
