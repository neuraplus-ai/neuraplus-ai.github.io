Perplexity AI Real-Time Search: 10 Examples That Show What It Can Do

PL
Prashant Lalwani2026-04-21 · 12 min read
Perplexity AIAI Search

The best way to understand what Perplexity AI can do is to see it in action. Here are 10 real-world queries that demonstrate its real-time search capability — and what makes each one impressive.

Try it free: Perplexity AI is available at perplexity.ai with a generous free tier. Pro plan ($20/month) unlocks unlimited Pro Search, GPT-4o and Claude models, and file upload analysis.

Example 1: Breaking News With Context

Query: "What happened in the Nvidia earnings call today?"
Perplexity fetches the latest news articles, earnings reports, and analyst commentary published in the last few hours, synthesises them into a summary with key numbers, and cites each source. A Google search would return headlines — Perplexity returns the answer.

Example 2: Live Stock or Crypto Data

Query: "What is Bitcoin's price right now and why did it move today?"
Perplexity pulls current price data and recent news explaining the movement — delivered as a single coherent paragraph. Saves 4–5 tab switches.

Example 3: Medical Research

Query: "What does the latest research say about metformin and longevity?"
Perplexity's Academic Focus mode searches PubMed, preprint servers, and medical journals published recently. You get a summarised research overview with study citations — without paying for journal access.

Example 4: Competitor Analysis

Query: "What features did Notion add in the last 3 months?"
Perplexity searches product update pages, changelogs, and tech blogs to compile a feature timeline. Perfect for competitive research without manually scanning multiple sites.

Example 5: Coding Error Diagnosis

Query: "Python 3.12 error: cannot import name 'Sequence' from 'collections' — fix?"
Perplexity searches current Stack Overflow threads, GitHub issues, and documentation to find the current fix — not a 2021 answer that may no longer apply.

Example 6: Local Business Research

Query: "Best highly-rated Indian restaurants opened in London in 2026?"
Recent reviews, food blog posts, and local news results are synthesised into a short recommendation list with sources.

Example 7: Travel Planning

Query: "Current visa requirements for Indian passport holders visiting Japan 2026?"
Perplexity fetches the official embassy page and recent traveller reports to give the current requirements — critical because visa rules change frequently.

Example 8: Academic Fact-Checking

Query: "Is it true that humans only use 10% of their brain? Cite sources."
Perplexity returns a direct debunking with citations from neuroscience publications — much faster than searching, reading, and verifying manually.

Example 9: Product Comparison

Query: "Compare the latest MacBook Pro M4 vs Dell XPS 15 for video editing — specs and reviews."
Perplexity compiles current specs, benchmark reviews, and user feedback into a side-by-side summary from multiple recent sources.

Example 10: Legal and Regulatory Updates

Query: "What are the latest AI regulations passed in the EU in 2026?"
Perplexity searches official EU publications, legal news sites, and tech policy blogs to give a current regulatory summary with official source links.

Pro tip: For the most accurate real-time results, use the Pro Search mode and select the appropriate Focus (News, Academic, or Web) depending on your query type.
