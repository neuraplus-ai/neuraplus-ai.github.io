2. Planning Your Website — The Brief That Changes Everything

The single most impactful thing you can do before building a website with Kimi AI has nothing to do with technology — it is writing a genuinely detailed brief that gives the model everything it needs to produce excellent first-draft output. Most users make the mistake of starting with a vague prompt like "build me a website for my business" and then spending hours in revision cycles that could have been avoided with five minutes of planning. The Kimi AI tutorial step by step guide covers the complete brief-writing process, but the essential elements include: your website's primary purpose and conversion goal, your target audience and their technical sophistication, your preferred color palette or brand colors, the specific sections and pages you need, any interactive features required, your hosting platform, and examples of websites whose aesthetic you admire. Providing this information upfront — even in bullet point form — consistently produces dramatically better first-draft output than iterating from a vague starting point. The investment of five minutes in brief writing typically saves thirty minutes of revision cycles, and for complex multi-page sites the time savings compound significantly.

3. The Complete Build Workflow — From Prompt to Deployed Site

Once your brief is ready, the actual process of building a website with Kimi AI follows a clear phase structure that experienced users have converged on after extensive experimentation. This workflow applies equally to complete beginners following the AI web builder for beginners guide and to experienced developers using Kimi for rapid prototyping. The key principle throughout is treating Kimi as a collaborative partner rather than a vending machine — providing context, reacting to output, and refining iteratively rather than expecting perfection from a single prompt.

Phase 1 — Brief & Generation (5 min)

Paste your complete brief into Kimi AI and request a full single-file HTML website. Ask for inline CSS and JS to keep deployment simple.

Phase 2 — Preview & Assess (5 min)

Save the output as index.html, open in browser. Assess layout, colors, responsiveness, and content accuracy holistically before requesting any changes.

Phase 3 — Targeted Revisions (10–20 min)

Request specific changes one section at a time. "Fix the navigation on mobile", "make the hero image full height", "add a pricing table after the features section".

Phase 4 — Content Refinement (10 min)

Replace placeholder content with your real text, images, and links. Ask Kimi to rewrite any section copy to match your brand voice.

Phase 5 — Deploy for Free (5 min)

Push to GitHub and enable GitHub Pages, or drag the file into Netlify Drop. Your Kimi-built website is live — no hosting fees, no platform subscription.

4. Advanced Techniques — Multi-Page Sites, SEO & Performance

Once you have mastered the single-page workflow, Kimi AI's capabilities for building websites extend significantly further than most users explore. Multi-page websites can be built by establishing a shared design system in the first conversation — asking Kimi to define your color variables, typography scale, navigation component, and footer — then generating each page in subsequent conversations that reference the established system. The free AI tools for web development guide contextualizes where Kimi fits alongside other tools like PageSpeed Insights, SEOptimer, and Lighthouse for validating the technical quality of your generated site. Kimi can be explicitly instructed to produce SEO-optimized output — including semantic HTML5 structure, proper heading hierarchies, descriptive alt attributes, canonical URL hints, and structured data markup — by including these requirements in your brief. Performance optimization is equally addressable: requesting that Kimi minimize unused CSS, lazy-load images, defer non-critical JavaScript, and use system font stacks produces output that scores significantly higher on Core Web Vitals than a naive first draft. The combination of Kimi AI website generation techniques with these advanced requirements consistently produces sites that would pass a professional technical audit without requiring manual post-processing.
