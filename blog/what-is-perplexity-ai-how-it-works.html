What Is Perplexity AI and How It Works: Complete Explanation

PL
Prashant Lalwani2026-04-10 · NeuraPulse
13 min readPerplexity AIHow It Works

If you have heard the name Perplexity AI everywhere in 2026 but are not sure exactly what it is — this guide explains everything clearly. Perplexity AI is an AI-powered answer engine that combines real-time web search with large language model synthesis to give you direct, cited answers rather than a list of links. Think of it as a search engine that actually reads the web for you.

🚀 The One-Line Summary: Perplexity AI is what you get when you combine Google's real-time web index with ChatGPT's language understanding — but with citations for every claim. It launched in 2022 and reached 100 million users by 2025.

What Is Perplexity AI?

Perplexity AI is an AI-powered search and answer engine founded in 2022 by Aravind Srinivas (CEO), Denis Yarats, Andy Konwinski, and Johnny Ho — all former researchers from OpenAI, Google, and DeepMind. Headquartered in San Francisco, the company raised over $1 billion in funding by 2025, reaching a valuation of approximately $9 billion.

The product is fundamentally different from both traditional search engines and AI chatbots:

  • Unlike Google: Instead of returning 10 links, Perplexity synthesizes information from multiple sources into one coherent answer with inline citations
  • Unlike ChatGPT: Instead of generating answers from training data that may be outdated, Perplexity searches the live web for every query — always current, always cited
  • Unlike Wikipedia: Perplexity answers any question dynamically, not just topics with Wikipedia articles, and answers are always current

For a direct comparison with Google Search, see our Perplexity AI vs Google Search comparison.

How Perplexity AI Works — The Architecture

Every query on Perplexity triggers a multi-stage process:

1

Query Processing

Your natural language question is processed by Perplexity's query understanding system. It identifies intent, key entities, and what type of answer is needed (factual, analytical, comparative, etc.).

2

Real-Time Web Search

Perplexity's search system (called "Sonar") crawls the live web, retrieving the most relevant and authoritative pages for your query. This includes news articles, academic papers, official websites, and high-quality blogs — indexed in real time.

3

Content Extraction and Ranking

The retrieved pages are parsed for relevant content. Perplexity's system extracts the specific sections most relevant to your query and ranks sources by authority, recency, and relevance.

4

LLM Synthesis

A large language model (Claude 3, GPT-4o, or Perplexity's own Sonar model) reads the extracted content and synthesizes a comprehensive, coherent answer. The LLM is instructed to stay grounded in the retrieved content — minimizing hallucination.

5

Citation Attribution

As the answer is generated, specific claims are attributed to their source document. These become the inline citation numbers [1][2][3] you see in Perplexity answers. Clicking a number opens a sidebar with the source URL and supporting snippet.

Which AI Models Does Perplexity Use?

Perplexity uses multiple AI models depending on the query type and user tier:

  • Sonar (Default): Perplexity's own fine-tuned model based on Llama architecture — optimized specifically for cited web search answers
  • Claude 3 (Anthropic): Available in Pro mode — excellent for nuanced analysis and long-form synthesis
  • GPT-4o (OpenAI): Available in Pro mode — strong for coding and complex reasoning
  • Sonar Pro: Perplexity's enhanced model for deeper research tasks

Pro users can select which model handles their queries. Free users get Sonar by default — which is well-optimized for search-grounded answers and performs well for most queries. See all features in our Perplexity AI features guide.

Perplexity AI vs ChatGPT

The most common comparison: both are AI chat interfaces but with completely different strengths. ChatGPT generates answers from its training data (knowledge cutoff), making it powerful for creative tasks, coding, and analysis of provided content, but unreliable for current events or facts that change over time. Perplexity searches the live web for every query, making it far more reliable for current factual questions but less creative than a standalone LLM.

Use Perplexity when you need: current information, citations, research synthesis, fact-checking. Use ChatGPT when you need: creative writing, code generation, analyzing documents you provide, brainstorming. Many power users use both daily for their respective strengths. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who created Perplexity AI?+

Perplexity AI was founded in 2022 by Aravind Srinivas (CEO, formerly at OpenAI and Google DeepMind), Denis Yarats (CTO), Andy Konwinski (co-founder of Databricks), and Johnny Ho. The company is headquartered in San Francisco and has raised over $1 billion in funding from investors including SoftBank, NEA, and Nvidia, reaching a $9 billion valuation by 2025.

Q: Is Perplexity AI safe to use?+

Yes — Perplexity AI is safe to use. The platform does not require personal information to start using it, and its citation system makes it easy to verify every claim. Like any AI tool, it can occasionally make errors — always verify critical information against primary sources. Perplexity has a clear privacy policy and does not sell user data to advertisers.

Q: How is Perplexity different from ChatGPT?+

The fundamental difference: ChatGPT generates answers from training data with a knowledge cutoff, while Perplexity searches the live web for every query. Perplexity cites its sources inline; ChatGPT typically does not. Perplexity excels at current information and fact-based research; ChatGPT excels at creative tasks, coding, and analysis. Both have their place in a modern AI workflow.
