Fun Google Tricks

Google has been hiding little jokes inside its search engine for over two decades. Type the right words into the search bar, and a plain results page turns into a spinning stunt, a tiny arcade game, or a wink at a 25-year-old video game line. Most of these tricks cost Google nothing to build and nothing to run, yet they've outlasted entire product launches.

We've rounded up seven of the best Google Easter eggs still worth trying in 2026 — some live directly on Google, others survive only on fan-run mirrors after Google quietly retired them. Each one below links to a full walkthrough with exact steps, the history behind it, and what to do if it doesn't trigger on the first try.

Games You Can Actually Play

Two of these aren't just visual gags — they're functioning mini-games hidden inside a search engine.

Zerg Rush

A StarCraft-inspired defense game where colorful "o" characters swarm in from the edges of the page and start eating your search results. Click them fast enough and you survive; let them through and the swarm spells out "GG." Read the full guide →

Atari Breakout

Search "Atari Breakout," hit Images, and your search results become the bricks in a fully playable version of the 1976 arcade classic. Guide coming soon.

Visual Gags That Break the Page

These don't require any clicking — just watch what happens to the layout.

  • Google Gravity — every search result, button, and logo letter loses gravity and crashes to the floor of the browser window.
  • Do a Barrel Roll — the entire page spins in a smooth 360° rotation and lands right back where it started.
  • Google Askew — the page tilts at an angle, like the whole browser window is slightly off its axis. Guide coming soon.
