The Anatomy of a Perfect Image Generation Prompt

Before we dive into specific templates, understand this framework. Every great prompt includes these elements:

1

Subject

What do you want to see? Be specific. Not "a person" but "a 30-year-old woman with curly red hair wearing a vintage leather jacket."

2

Action/Context

What's happening? "Sitting at a café" vs "working on a laptop at a sunlit café in Paris."

3

Style/Art Direction

Photorealistic, oil painting, cyberpunk, minimalist, watercolor, etc. Reference specific artists or movements.

4

Lighting & Atmosphere

Golden hour, studio lighting, moody, dramatic, soft natural light, neon glow.

5

Technical Details

Camera specs (85mm lens, f/1.8), resolution (8K, 4K), quality markers (photorealistic, hyper-detailed).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️ Don't Make These Errors
  • Being too vague: "A nice picture" won't work. Be specific.
  • Contradictory terms: Don't say "minimalist" and "highly detailed" together.
  • Too many elements: Focus on 3-5 key details, not 20.
  • Forgetting lighting: Lighting makes or breaks an image. Always specify it.
  • Ignoring aspect ratio: Specify --ar 16:9 or similar for the right composition.
💡 Pro Tip: The Iteration Method

Start with a basic prompt, then refine based on results. Add or remove elements gradually. Save your best prompts in a library for reuse. I maintain a document of 100+ tested prompts that I can quickly adapt for new projects.
